# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RunHop serves two primary user groups.

The first group is runners and cyclists, especially people who care about serious training, real-world events, and connecting with other committed athletes. They use the product to discover races, register for events, join communities, and find other people to train or participate with.

The second group is race organizers and organizations that want a trustworthy platform where they can publish races, present event details clearly, manage registration demand, and sell related offerings such as race entries and merchandise.

## Product Purpose

RunHop exists to connect the participation side of endurance sports with the organizing side. For athletes, it should make it easy to find credible races, register confidently, and participate in a more serious community around running and cycling. For organizers, it should function as a dependable event presence where their races can be presented professionally and where registrations and related offerings can be sold with confidence.

The product should feel useful both as a social platform for athletes who want to run together and as a commerce and discovery platform for race organizers who want their events to be taken seriously.

## Brand Personality

Serious, disciplined, premium, classy.

The voice should feel established and credible rather than trendy or playful. It should communicate trust, precision, and performance-minded confidence. The product should feel built for committed runners and cyclists, not for casual novelty or gamified wellness culture.

Reference direction:

- Ferrari, for cinematic confidence, sharp discipline, and premium restraint
- Bugatti, for seriousness, precision, and established luxury presence

## Anti-references

RunHop should not feel like:

- Meta-style product branding or interface tone
- MongoDB-style visual or brand personality
- bubbly, soft, or childlike fitness products
- Fitbit-like playful wellness positioning
- Google-like generic, rounded, or overly friendly consumer softness

It should avoid feeling toy-like, casual, cute, rounded, or socially noisy.

## Design Principles

- Build trust through precision. The product should feel deliberate, controlled, and operationally credible for both athletes and organizers.
- Treat competition and participation seriously. The interface should reflect real commitment to races, training culture, and event execution rather than casual lifestyle content.
- Let organizers present events with authority. Race listings, registration flows, and event surfaces should feel professional enough for organizations to trust as a sales and reputation channel.
- Keep the social layer performance-minded. Community features should support serious athletes finding people, groups, and events, without drifting into playful social-app behavior.
- Premium does not mean decorative. The experience should feel refined and established, using restraint and clarity rather than excess ornament.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

RunHop should meet WCAG AA as the baseline accessibility standard.

Accessibility decisions should preserve trust and usability for a broad range of users, including athletes and organizers using the product in varied real-world conditions. The interface should prioritize readable content, clear hierarchy, robust focus states, and interaction patterns that remain usable without relying on visual flourish or ambiguity.
